Drill:

  • Which one properly defines an array?
  • What are the values in the array?
  • What are the types of values in the array?

1. var my_array = {"one", 2, "three"};

2. var my_array = ["one", 2, "three"];

3. var my_array ("one", 2, "three");

4. var my_array = ["one", 2, [3, 3.5], "four"];

  • The 2nd and 4th arrays are correct. The 1st and 3rd arrays are ot correct.
  • In the 2nd array, the values are the word "one", the number 2 (two) and the word "three". In the 4th array, the values are the word "one", the number 2 (two), a child array containing the numbers 3 and 3.5, and the word "four" (not part of the child array).
  • The words are called strings. The numerals are just numbers, and in the 4th array there is an array within it.
